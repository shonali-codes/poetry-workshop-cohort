Module 10 · Week Six · The Closing

Fuelling Innovation & Creative Thinking

Poetry as a Cognitive Superpower
DateSaturday, 6 June 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention Not to teach. To exchange. Each of us brings something we have created across these six weeks. I bring something too. We respond to each other's work — not as critics, but as fellow witnesses. This is the closing of our journey.

Before we meet

This is the assignment I gave you at the very start of the workshop. By today, you will have carried a quiet background question for six weeks:

What is this workshop making me want to make?

Today is the answer. Bring whatever you have created. It does not have to be a poem. It can be a painting, a song, a piece of code, a recipe, a garment, a letter, a short film, a photograph, a choreography, a ritual, a small object, or anything else that arose in you across these six weeks. It does not have to be polished. It has to be honest.

I will also bring something I made — because I am also being changed by this workshop. We will respond to each other's work together.

What we will do together

Tonight we do not learn. We exchange.

  1. A welcome and framing — tonight is an answer, not a class.
  2. A brief settling — a few breaths, a moment of quiet.
  3. Two of us present our creations, in turn — with brief contextualising of what arose. Held silence after each. A response from me, a response from the cohort.
  4. I share what I made — in the middle of the circle, as a bridge between those who have just shared and those yet to share.
  5. The remaining two of us present, in turn — same rhythm, same attention.
  6. A brief mention of what comes next — Poetry Spaces events and future seasons of this workshop.
  7. A closing line of poetry, read aloud. Then silence. Then thank you.
A note on time. This is the final session of a six-week journey. If the exchange runs a little long, let it. Ending well matters more than ending on the minute.

After our closing

In the week after Module 10, you will hear from me privately — a personal note of thanks, and any final pieces of feedback on what you have shared with me across the workshop. You will also hear about what comes next — future Poetry Spaces events and the seasons that follow this one.
