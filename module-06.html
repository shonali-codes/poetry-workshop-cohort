Module 06 · Week Four

Writing Original Poetry

Finding Your Voice
DateSaturday, 23 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To sit with each other as fellow poets — not as teacher and students — and share honestly how we come to the page. To let each of us submit our first poem as an act of trust, not performance.

Before we meet

Before class, sit quietly for a few minutes and ask yourself: what pulls me to the page? what blocks me? what surprises me about my own writing? what am I still figuring out? You do not have to write these answers down. Simply sit with them.

Today, each of us will take a turn sharing our own approach to writing. I will share mine too. This is not teaching. It is four poets in a quiet room.

What we will do together

Tonight we are four poets sitting together. I am not teaching you how to write. I am sharing how I come to the page, and listening to how you come to yours.

  1. A welcome — the frame for the evening. Listening, not instruction.
  2. Each of you shares — roughly ten minutes each. What pulls you to the page, what blocks you, what surprises you about your own writing, what you are still figuring out.
  3. A brief pause.
  4. I share — honestly. What changes across 25 years of writing. What Rabindra Sangeet taught me about the body of a line. What BhashaSetu taught me about writing across tongues. What I am still figuring out.
  5. An open conversation — where our approaches meet, where they diverge, what each of us might try from the other's method.
  6. Optional writing time — offered, not required. A few quiet minutes for anyone who feels moved to write.
  7. Framing the first submission — the poem you have been carrying around, wishing someone would really read.

The invitation you will carry home

For your first poem submission — one of the gifts of this cohort experience — I would love to read a poem you have already written. Not one you will write this week. Choose the piece from your existing work that you have been carrying around, wishing someone would really read. Bring me the poem you trust me with. I will respond with detailed written feedback within a week.
