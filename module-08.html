Module 08 · Week Five

Poetry as Mindful Practice

Presence Through Language
DateSaturday, 30 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To move from talking about mindful practice to doing it — and to discover that a poem received in meditation lands differently than a poem received in analysis.

Before we meet

Come with a small notebook and a pen if you can. You will not need them during the session itself, but you will carry them into the week that follows.

If you can, sit somewhere quiet during the class. Close other windows. This is a receiving session, not a scrolling one.

What we will do together

Tonight we do not study mindful practice. We practise it. A few minutes of anulom-vilom, then Soumitra Chatterjee's recitation of a Gitanjali poem, then silence, then a gentle conversation. No analysis is required. Only receiving.

  1. A brief welcome and framing — tonight we do not study, we practise.
  2. A brief introduction to anulom-vilom, for any of us who have not done it before.
  3. Anulom-vilom together — a few minutes of alternate-nostril breathing.
  4. A settling silence — no instruction, only arrival.
  5. Soumitra Chatterjee's recitation of a Gitanjali poem — listened to together, eyes closed if you wish.
  6. Held silence after the recitation — let it land where it lands.
  7. A gentle return — opening eyes, settling back into the room.
  8. A quiet conversation — I share first, briefly, what I noticed. You are welcome to share anything you noticed, or to stay quiet. Both are valid.
  9. A brief teaching on poetry as daily practice — and the framing of the week-long invitation that follows.
A note on participation. You are welcome to listen without sharing. You are welcome to sit through the meditation without doing the week-long practice. The structure is held. The threshold of your disclosure is yours.

The invitation you will carry home

For the week ahead, I invite you — if it feels right — to do this practice daily. Anulom-vilom, then sit and listen to the same Gitanjali recitation, then hold silence. Each day, after the practice, write one or two sentences in a small notebook — not an analysis, just a noting of what you noticed. Bring the week's notes to our next class if you wish to share them, or keep them entirely private. The notebook is for you, not for me. What matters is what you notice, not what you produce.
Gitanjali recitation (for daily practice) Share notebook reflections (optional) Share feedback on this class
Sharing policy: private. Contemplative notebooks are personal. No participant is required to share. If you do choose to share, only I will read.
What we just did was shravana — hearing. The week ahead is manana — quiet reflection on what was heard.
