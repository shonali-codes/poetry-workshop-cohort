Module 05 · Week Three

History of Poems & Poets

Poetry as Civilisational Memory
DateSaturday, 16 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To travel three and a half millennia of poetic lineage through a single thread — what is worth remembering, and who remembers it? — and to honour our Patron's entry into that lineage by name.

Before we meet

Today is a journey across three and a half thousand years of poetry. We will not cover it all. We will follow a single thread — a question that every era has answered differently. Come rested. You will be receiving more than producing today.

What we will do together

Today we travel from the Rig Veda to the spoken word stage — not as a checklist, but as a thread. Each era is a witness to the same question, asked differently.

  1. A welcome and the thread — what is worth remembering, and who remembers it?
  2. The Rig Veda moment — hymns as the first act of preservation. What a civilisation at its beginning chose to remember.
  3. The classical Sanskrit moment — Valmiki, Vyasa, Kalidasa. Memory of dharma, of beauty, of rasa.
  4. The Bhakti moment — memory shifts from Sanskrit to the vernaculars, from priests to farmers and women. Kabir, Mirabai, Akka Mahadevi, Tukaram.
  5. A brief pause.
  6. The Persian-Urdu moment — the ghazal in India. Memory of love and loss as civilisational witness.
  7. The Bengali Renaissance moment — Tagore, Nazrul. Indigenous voice recovered against colonial erasure.
  8. The contemporary moment — Amrita Pritam, Kamala Das, Mahadevi Varma, spoken word today. What our contemporaries are preserving for tomorrow.
  9. The named acknowledgement — our Patron is honoured by name, entering the lineage we have walked tonight.
  10. A closing blessing — and the invitation of a rest week ahead.

The week ahead — rest

There is no homework this week. No writing prompt, no analysis to return. The week ahead is a rest week — our Sunday 17 May is quiet.

Let what we touched tonight settle where it wants to. If any one era or poem from tonight keeps returning to you across the week, notice that — it is the lineage choosing you, not you choosing it. Rest before we return.
