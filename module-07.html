Module 07 · Week Four

Encapsulating History in Verse

The Poet as Witness
DateSunday, 24 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To understand that history is never witnessed from one position — that two poets writing about the same event can produce opposite truths, and that poetry's witness lives in the gap between them.

Before we meet

No readings required in advance. Come with one question held quietly — what, in my own life or time, have I witnessed that no one else can witness quite the same way? You do not have to answer it yet. Carry it with you into the class.

What we will do together

Poetry carries what history books cannot hold. Tonight we listen to two witnesses of the same moment — and discover that they remembered differently.

  1. A welcome and framing — poetry as the keeper of what history books cannot hold.
  2. The first historical witness poem — read aloud, briefly unpacked. What did this poet see, what were they inside of?
  3. The second witness, from the same moment — different vantage, different wound. The gap between them is the teaching.
  4. A brief pause.
  5. My own witness poem — read aloud. Briefly shared. Honest rather than dazzling. Evidence that the facilitator also does this work.
  6. A cohort conversation — what kinds of historical material does each of you feel drawn to? Past or present, equally valid.
  7. A closing — framing the homework, and noting that a resource pack on self-publishing is being released by email after class.

The invitation you will carry home

Write your own witness poem. About anything historic — past or present. It does not have to be about a great event. The disappearance of a small thing is also history. Write about whatever you alone are positioned to witness.
