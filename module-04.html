Module 04 · Week Two

Learning About Poets & Their Lives

The Human Behind the Verse
DateSunday, 10 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To witness how biography shapes verse — and to discover that both legend and documentation are ways of knowing a poet, each with their own truth.

Before we meet

Today we move through the lives of two poets separated by fifteen centuries — Kalidasa, the classical Sanskrit master about whom we know almost nothing that is documented, and Rabindranath Tagore, about whom we know almost everything. The contrast is itself the teaching.

For the Tagore portion of this module, we will take one of two paths — contemplative and inward, or outward and world-building. The choice is made in advance by the group, so the session has its chosen shape when we gather.

What we will do together

Today we ask: how do we know a poet? Through their verse alone, or through their life and verse together? And does the difference change how we read them?

  1. A brief welcome and framing of the question — how do we know a poet?
  2. An overview of Kalidasa — the legend of the cowherd transformed by Kali's grace, the works, what poetic tradition tells us regardless of literal truth.
  3. A brief reading from Kalidasa — one stanza, held attentively.
  4. A brief pause.
  5. Tagore — through the path we have chosen together. Either his early years and how wounds became the foundations of his poetic voice; or his vision of Santiniketan, the poet as builder of the world he wished had held him.
  6. A reading of one Tagore poem that meets us in the chosen register.
  7. The bridge — how both poets, across fifteen centuries, moved through difficulty into transformative voice. Poetry as survival and as gift.
  8. A gentle cohort reflection to close.

The invitation you will carry home

A gentle invitation. Choose one early memory — not necessarily painful, simply early. Write a short piece from the perspective of the child you were. Not about what happened. Just what the child saw, heard, felt. You do not need to share it with anyone. Submit it only if you want to. The writing alone is the point.
