Module 01 · Week One

Analysing Classical & Modern Poetry

The Art of Deep Reading
DateSaturday, 2 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To introduce deep reading as an inherited craft — a way of listening to a poem three ways: for story, for sound, and for what it does to the reader when the page is put down.

Before we meet

No preparation is required for our first session. Arrive as you are. I will share the three-poem triad with you in our first class — two we will read together, and one I will place in your hands as the week's gentle homework.

If you wish, sit quietly for a few minutes before the session begins. Clear your desk. Have water within reach. Arrive at 3:30 PM IST having already settled into the register we will meet in.

What we will do together

Our first session opens with a few minutes on how we will walk these six weeks as a cohort — the structure, the safety, the invitation to explore yourself inside a held space. After that, we enter the poems.

We will deep-read two poems together from different traditions. Between them, a brief pause to breathe. At the end, one poem is offered as homework for the week — to sit with privately, and to read three times before writing anything about it.

  1. A brief opening framing on how we will walk these six weeks together — the co-learner tone, the structure, the safety, and the quiet background question that Module 10 will ask us to answer.
  2. A short introduction to the three-poem triad — three poems, three traditions, one question asked three ways: what does it mean to give, to lose, and to return?
  3. Deep reading of the first poem — unhurried, layered, holding space for the Bengali original and its English translation side by side.
  4. A brief pause — water, breath, regather.
  5. Companion reading of the second poem — from a different tradition, in conversation with the first, letting the two speak to each other.
  6. A closing reflection on how the two poems bridge toward the third — which will wait for you at home.

The invitation you will carry home

At the close of the session, you will receive the third poem of the triad — the one we do not unpack together. Your invitation for the week is this:

Sit with this poem alone this week — not with me. Read it once for the story, once for the sound, once for what it does to you when you put the page down. Then write your own analysis — in two or three paragraphs, or as long as you need. Submit it through the link that will come by email. After all four of us have submitted, I will share my own reading — alongside anonymised excerpts from the cohort's readings, if you consent. This is how we begin to read together.
