Module 09 · Week Five

Bridging Cultures & Generations

Poetry as Living Connection
DateSunday, 31 May 2026
Time3:30 PM IST
Duration1.5 hours
Our intention To see poetry as a bridge — and to meet one of the most extraordinary bridges ever built, Tagore's Chitrangada, which carries a two-thousand-year-old Manipuri princess from the Mahabharata into modern feminist consciousness.

Before we meet

By now you have submitted your second poem, written out of the Module 08 meditation week. Full written feedback will reach you separately by email.

Come with your meditation notebook if you kept one — we will gently touch into what the week held, only to the depth you wish to share.

What we will do together

Tonight we meet one of the most extraordinary bridges ever built in poetry. A story that belongs to no one — not to Bengal, not to our generation, not even to any single century — and yet has been made to carry meaning across all of them.

  1. A welcome and the bridge — what Chitrangada itself enacts at every level.
  2. Brief sharing from the Module 08 meditation week — invitation to share one or two things, or to pass. Both are valid.
  3. Brief acknowledgement of the second poem submissions received — full feedback comes separately.
  4. The Chitrangada talk — the original Mahabharata story, Tagore's 1892 verse-play, the 1936 dance-drama, why Tagore returned to her after 44 years.
  5. The bridges this single work has crossed — geography, language, time, gender politics, dance traditions.
  6. A closing reflection — what will you carry across?

No homework — only quiet preparation

There is no written homework for this module. The week ahead is quiet preparation. For our final session on the 6th of June — Module 10 — bring whatever wants to be made. See the Module 10 page for what that closing exchange looks like.
